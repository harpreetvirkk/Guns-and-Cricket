Politics and Terror stifle Pakistak's Game

Pakistan’s ODI wins have been on a constant dipping spree for the last decade, despite the 180-run sweep last summer, that remains a stinging sore for the Indians. Tensed bilateral ties, a weak bureaucracy and a notoriously bad reputation cost them the home-ground advantage.

The India-Pakistan arch-rivalry has been a skirmish of identity and validation. The sentiments attached with every The Indian team has had no shortage of neither support nor validation. Turns out, that India’s winning streak is fairly a modern phenomenon: Influenced heavily by what happens across the barbed wires and outside the setting of billions of Indians and Pakistanis glued to their TV screens.

For Pakistan, the sheen of the glorious 15 years of ODI dominance, is now on the verge of wiping out of existence. Here’s why.

Between 1985 and 2005, Pakistan maintained a winning streak of 52 matches out of a total of 80 matches. What is interesting though, is that the organisation of these matches have been fairly scattered, spiking to a top of ten matches played in 1997 and fairly fluctuating around the same ballpark.

The turn of the century saw a series of political speculations and ceasefire violations that strained bilateral ties, and reflected onto the ODIs. The years that stand out in the fluctuations are 1989-90, 2000-2004, 2008 and onwards.
